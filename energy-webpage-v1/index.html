Appliance Energy Consumption

Explore how everyday household appliances consume energy in Australian homes. Understanding energy usage helps reduce electricity bills and environmental impact.

Why energy consumption matters

Australian households spend an average of $1,700 per year on electricity. Appliances account for a significant portion of this cost. By understanding which appliances use the most energy, you can make smarter choices.

🏠

Average household

An average Australian home uses around 18–20 kWh of electricity per day across all appliances.

💡

Biggest energy users

Heating and cooling, water heaters, and refrigerators are the top energy consumers in most homes.

📉

Reduce your bill

Switching to energy-efficient appliances with high star ratings can cut energy costs by up to 30%.

Television Energy Consumption

Televisions are one of the most common appliances in Australian homes. Learn how different TV types and sizes affect your energy bill.

TV types and energy use

📺

LED TV

Most common type today. A 55-inch LED TV uses around 60–80 watts. Energy efficient and widely available in Australia.

🖥️

OLED TV

Superior picture quality but uses slightly more power — around 80–120 watts for a 55-inch screen.

📡

Plasma TV (old)

Older plasma TVs used 150–300 watts — much higher than modern equivalents. Most have been phased out.
